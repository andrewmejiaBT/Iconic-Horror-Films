Iconic Horror Films

Welcome to my iconic horror film blog, where we will be talking about three famous horror films!

Friday the 13th

Friday the 13th is about a group of young camp counselors who decide to set up and settle for the summer in Camp Crystal Lake. They soon discover the camp isn't just sunshine and rainbows, as a merciless killer stalkes them, slashing, stabbing and hunting them as they struggle to stay alive.

The film is considered to be a slasher-horror film. It released in theaters on May 9, 1980 across the U.S., earning $5,816,321 in its opening weekend, and becoming the 18th highest-grossing film in 1980. The film has spawned a dozen sequels, a tv series, books, merchandise, and even a video game.

The Shining

The Shining is about a writer named Jack Torrance who becomes a caretaker of the isolated Overlook Hotel in Colorado, aspiring to overcome his writer's block. With his wife and son, Jack settles into the eerily quiet hotel. That isn't until his son begins to have disturbing visions, and with Jack's writing not coming together, Jack soon discovers the hotel's harrowing secrets. His family becomes terrified of him, as he unwinds into a homicidal maniac, terrorizing them.

The film is considered to be a psychological horror film. It released in theaters on May 23, 1980, but was only released on 10 screens. Though it had little screenings, it grossed $622,337 for its four day weekend, making it the third highest-grossing opening weekend with fewer than 50 screens of all time, right behind Star Wars. It's impact on pop culture is vast, particulary in films, tv shows, video games, music, and even other films.

Alien

Alien is about the crew of the starship, Nostromo, when they are awakened from their cyro-sleep pods midway through their destination. They answer to a distress signal from an alien vessel, only to find horror nested inside. One of the crew encounters a nest of eggs inside the alien vessel, when a creature inside the egg jumps out and attaches itself to the crew member, putting them into a coma.

The film is considered to be a science-fiction horror film. It released in theaters on May 25, 1979 in the U.S., grossing $3,527,881 over its four-day weekend with a per-screen average of $38,767. In just its first four weeks, it already grossed $16.5 million. Alien's cultural impact was both short-term and long-term, but more importantly it paved the way for other science-fiction horror films such as Predator (1987).
